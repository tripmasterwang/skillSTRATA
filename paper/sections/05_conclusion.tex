In this work, we introduced \textbf{SkillStrata}, a stratified skill \textbf{system} that curates trace-to-skill evolution into a governed three-layer graph and assembles task-fit skills on demand.
By \textbf{curating} a \textbf{Skill Strata} graph through refactor-style \textsc{Split}, dependency-aware \textsc{Route}, and a \textbf{propose-then-verify} gate, then assembling skills like LEGO at test time, SkillStrata transcends the \textbf{monolithic} and \textbf{flat-bank} paradigms.
Our experiments showcase \textbf{\ding{182} the highest task success at the lowest loaded-token cost, \ding{183} that splitting and routing, not amassing skills, drive these gains, \ding{184} a real-benchmark validation gate that autonomously rejects a harmful batch yet accepts a beneficial one, and \ding{185} test-time assembly that recovers out-of-domain coverage from trace sub-parts.}
These results suggest a \textbf{promising path toward self-evolving agents} that govern their own skills rather than merely amass them.
\textbf{More broadly, shifting from ever-larger skill documents to a governed stratified system gives self-evolving libraries an operating-system-style discipline that gates curation and execution alike.}
Future directions include completing the in-progress real-benchmark head-to-head, scaling to larger libraries, and integrating the verify-loop into broader agent stacks.
